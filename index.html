Start Here ‚Äî The Vocabulary

Before any code, internalize these terms. Your AS team uses them daily.

BM25
The baseline ranking algorithm. Scores documents by term frequency + rarity. Still used as first-stage retrieval in Amazon Search.
Learning to Rank (LTR)
Using ML to re-rank results. Trains on human judgments or click data. Pointwise / Pairwise / Listwise approaches.
NDCG
Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain. The primary offline metric for ranking quality. Higher = better relevance at top positions.
Feature Engineering
Crafting signals for the model: BM25 score, click CTR, query-title match, price, recency, seller rating, etc.
Judgment / Label
Human-assigned relevance score (0-4 scale: perfect/good/fair/bad/irrelevant) for a query-product pair. The ground truth.
LambdaMART
The most common LTR algorithm in production. Gradient boosted trees optimized for ranking metrics. Fast at inference.
Two-Stage Ranking
Stage 1: BM25/ANN retrieves top-K candidates fast. Stage 2: ML re-ranks them with expensive features. How Amazon Search works.
Dense Retrieval / Embeddings
Semantic search using neural embeddings. Finds "running shoes" for query "sneakers for jogging" even without exact term match.
ESCI Labels
Amazon's own relevance taxonomy: Exact, Substitute, Complement, Irrelevant. Used in Amazon's public shopping queries dataset.
Click-Through Rate (CTR)
Implicit signal. What users click becomes training data. Biased by position (top results get more clicks regardless of quality).
